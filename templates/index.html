C

Criticality

How vital is the asset to operations? How severe would the impact be if it were lost or destroyed?

—
/ 5
Minimal1Minimal or no impact on operations (DR Priority 4 & 5)
Minor2Minor impact/inconvience to operations (DR Priority 3)
Moderate3Moderate Impact on operations (DR Priority 2)
Major4Significant impact on business operations (DR Priority 1)
Critical5Loss would cause dramatic impact on business operations (DR Priority 0)
Please select a score for Criticality.
A

Accessibility

How easily can an adversary physically or digitally reach and access the target?

—
/ 5
Minimal1Extremely difficult to access (e.g., segmented network, air-gapped, disconnected from main network)
Minor2Difficult to access (e.g., controlled enclave, restricted ACL)
Moderate3Accessible but protected (e.g., behind firewall, creds + MFA)
Major4Accessible but monitored (.e.g., activity logging, credentials but no MFA)
Critical5Fully public; no meaningful access controls
Please select a score for Accessibility.
R

Recoverability

How much time, effort, and resources are required to restore or replace the asset after an attack?

—
/ 5
Minor1Immediately replacable, restorable, or redundancies in place (DR Priority 4 & 5)
Minimal2Minimal impact and downtime, reset credentials (DR Priority 3)
Moderate3Delayed restoration or replacement (DR Priority 2)
Major4Significant impact on business continuity (DR Priority 1)
Critical5Dramatic impact on business continuity (DR Priority 0)
Please select a score for Recoverability.

R²

Recognizability

Can an adversary easily identify the target and distinguish it from other assets?

—
/ 5
Minimal1System is obfuscated or unique and customized. Recognized and known only by a few individuals

Major4Easily recognized or identified with limited technical expertise (e.g., dig, nslookup)
